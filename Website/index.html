The Curator's Compendium

An archive of worlds that endure, stories that linger, and characters that live on.

Welcome to my personal collection. This compendium is a living document of the media I consider foundationalâ€”the works that have shaped my perspective, defined genres, or simply stayed with me long after the credits rolled. This is not a list of reviews, but a tribute to the creators and the resonant worlds they've built.

Video Games

Elden Ring

FromSoftware's magnum opus. The Lands Between is a staggering achievement, a true open-world that trusts the player to discover its haunting beauty, brutal lore, and earth-shattering challenges without guidance.

Bloodborne

A chilling masterpiece of gothic and cosmic horror. Yharnam is a suffocating, labyrinthine world that rewards aggression. This is a descent into madness, trading shields for speed in a fast-paced hunt for the eldritch truth.

Sekiro

A punishingly precise story of honor and resurrection. Sekiro revolutionized melee combat, demanding mastery not of attrition, but of posture and deflection. It is a rhythmic clash of steel that is one of the most satisfying experiences in gaming.

Slay the Princess

A deceptively simple premise that unfolds into a brilliant psychological horror meta-narrative. A story about choice, paradox, and the very nature of storytelling, anchored by a phenomenal voice performance and haunting artwork.

Horizon Zero Dawn

A breathtaking vision of a 'post-post-apocalyptic' world reclaimed by nature and ruled by magnificent mechanical beasts. This is world-building at its finest, blending tribal sci-fi with a deeply compelling mystery about the fall of the 'Old Ones'.

Hollow Knight

A sprawling, hand-drawn Metroidvania masterpiece. The ruined kingdom of Hallownest is vast, melancholic, and packed with secrets. Its precise platforming, challenging combat, and subtly-told lore create an unforgettable, epic adventure.

Literature

Lord of the Rings

The foundational epic of modern fantasy. Tolkien's work is a masterpiece of linguistics, mythology, and profound storytelling. More than a story, it is a complete secondary world exploring fellowship, industrial decay, and the persistent struggle against encroaching darkness.

Iteration 110: Cradle

The peak of progression fantasy. Will Wight's series is dangerously addictive, following Lindon on a relentless journey from the weakest of his clan to a cosmic power. It's pure, satisfying advancement backed by a fantastic magic system.

Harry Potter Series

The defining cultural phenomenon for a generation. Rowling built a magical world that feels like home, perfectly capturing the wonder and terror of growing up. It's a timeless coming-of-age story layered with deep themes of love, prejudice, and sacrifice.

Sherlock Holmes

The quintessential deductive mind. Doyle's iconic detective set the unbreakable blueprint for all crime fiction that followed. Holmes is not just a character; he is an archetype, a cold engine of logic navigating the fog-lit streets of Victorian London.

The Hunger Games

A brutal and brilliant dystopian saga for the modern era. Collins masterfully interrogates our obsession with reality TV, systemic oppression, and the real-world consequences of violence, all wrapped in a narrative that is impossible to put down.

Percy Jackson

Rick Riordan's masterful fusion of ancient Greek mythology and the awkwardness of the modern world. This series is defined by its sharp humor, relentless adventure, and deep heart, making millennia-old gods and monsters feel relevant and real.

Web Series & Movies

Arcane

A landmark in western animation. Fortiche's breathtaking 'oil-painting' art style serves a deeply mature, character-driven tragedy. A heartbreaking story of two sisters torn apart by class, ideology, and trauma in the steampunk dystopia of Piltover and Zaun.

The Blacklist

A captivating, high-stakes crime thriller completely anchored by James Spader's magnetic performance. His portrayal of the enigmatic 'concierge of crime,' Raymond Reddington, is a masterclass in ambiguity, charm, and menace that elevates the entire series.

Castlevania

The series that broke the 'video game curse.' A dark, violent, and gorgeously animated medieval fantasy that treats its source material with maturity and respect, crafting a witty, bloody, and surprisingly philosophical tale of vampires, hunters, and heretics.

Loki

A brilliant, time-bending dive into bureaucracy and existentialism. This series takes the MCU's most charismatic villain and forces him to confront his own nature, giving the God of Mischief unexpected philosophical depth, agency, and purpose.

V for Vendetta

A potent and stylish political thriller adapted from Alan Moore's masterpiece. It's a powerful statement about individual autonomy versus state control, and the enduring, dangerous power of an idea to dismantle an oppressive regime. Voila!

Chernobyl

A harrowing, meticulous masterpiece of historical dramatization. This is pure, radiological horror that serves as a terrifying testament to the human cost of systemic lies and the bureaucracy of denial. A difficult but absolutely essential watch.

Characters

The Endless

From Neil Gaiman's *Sandman*. They are not gods; they are anthropomorphic personifications of reality's building blocks. A dysfunctional family of concepts: Destiny, Death, Dream, Destruction, Desire, Despair, and Delirium.

Doctor Doom

Comics' greatest villain. The monarch of Latveria is a tragic figure of unparalleled intellect, unmatched arrogance, and indomitable will. A master of science and sorcery, Doom genuinely believes his absolute rule is the only path to salvation for humanity.

Lucifer

The original rebel. As conceived by Gaiman, the Lightbringer is not a monster, but a cosmic aesthete. Bored with his throne in Hell, he abandoned it to seek absolute free will, explore creation, and run a piano bar in Los Angeles.

John Constantine

The quintessential working-class warlock. A chain-smoking, trench-coated cynic who survives the dark arts not through power, but through guile, manipulation, and a conscience he'd much rather ignore. He is the master of the magical con.

Ghost Rider

The Spirit of Vengeance. A terrifying demonic entity, the wrath of God bound to a mortal host. The Ghost Rider is a supernatural engine of retribution, tasked with punishing the guilty with the burning hellfire of the Penance Stare.

Etrigan

Created by Jack Kirby. A powerful demon from Hell bound by Merlin to the mortal Jason Blood. With the famous chantâ€”'Gone, gone, the form of man! Rise, the Demon Etrigan!'â€”the two swap places, unleashing a rhyming agent of chaos.

V

The masked revolutionary from Alan Moore's *V for Vendetta*. V is not a person but a living idea; an anarchist symbol of rebellion who uses theatricality, violence, and the legacy of Guy Fawkes to dismantle a neo-fascist state.
